RELATIVE PRONOUNS

  • Who: Refers to people or animals
  • Whom: Refers to object (often is people)
  • Whose: Refers to possession
  • Which: Refers to animals or things
  • That: Refers to people, animals or things
  • Where: Refers to place
  • When: Refers to time
  • Why: Refers to reason

Who and Whom

Use Case Example
"Who" Used as the Subject of a Sentence "Who made this delicious cake?"
"Who" Used as the Subject of a Clause "The student who won the prize is very talented."
"Who" Used as the Subject Performing the Action "I know the person who called you."
"Whom" Used as the Object of a Verb "The teacher whom the students respect is retiring."
"Whom" Used as the Object of a Preposition "The friend with whom I went to the concert is moving away."
"Whom" Used as the Object Receiving the Action "To whom did you send the letter?"
